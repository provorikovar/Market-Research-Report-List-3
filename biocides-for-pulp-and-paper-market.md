<p><p>Biocides for pulp and paper market types can be divided into oxidizing and non-oxidizing categories. Oxidizing biocides work by releasing active oxygen atoms to disrupt cell structures and eliminate microorganisms like bacteria and fungi. Non-oxidizing biocides, on the other hand, work by penetrating cell membranes and interfering with cellular processes. Both types of biocides are crucial in the pulp and paper industry to prevent microbial growth, control slime formation, and maintain product quality. Choosing the right type of biocide depends on specific application requirements and efficacy against target microorganisms.</p></p>
