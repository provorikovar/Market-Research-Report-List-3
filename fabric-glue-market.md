<p><p>Fabric glue comes in two main types, permanent and temporary. Permanent fabric glue is designed for strong, long-lasting bonds that will withstand washing and wear. It is ideal for attaching patches, hems, and appliques to fabric. Temporary fabric glue, on the other hand, is meant for temporary bonds that can be easily removed or washed out. This type of glue is often used for temporary fixes or for positioning fabric pieces before sewing them together. Both types serve different purposes in the fabric crafting market.</p></p>
