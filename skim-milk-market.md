Pasteurized skim milk undergoes a process of heat treatment to eliminate bacteria and extend its shelf life, while UHT skim milk is heated to a much higher temperature for a shorter time, resulting in a longer shelf life without refrigeration. Both types offer consumers a low-fat alternative to whole milk while maintaining essential nutrients.</p></p>
