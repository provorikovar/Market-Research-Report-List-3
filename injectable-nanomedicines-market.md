<p><p>Injectable nanomedicines are categorized into various types based on their composition and structure. </p><p>Liposomes are spherical vesicles composed of phospholipid bilayers, used for delivering drugs to specific target sites. Micelles are nanosized aggregates formed by amphiphilic molecules, enabling solubilization of hydrophobic drugs. Nanocrystals are solid particles of drug that exhibit high surface area and high drug loading capacity. Polymeric nanoparticles are synthetic particles made of biocompatible polymers, used for controlled drug release. Other types of nanomedicines include dendrimers, nanogels, and quantum dots.</p></p>
