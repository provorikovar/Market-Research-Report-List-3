<p><p>Magnetic switches come in two main types: Reed Type and Hall Effect Type. Reed Type switches use a set of enclosed contacts that are opened or closed by a magnetic field produced by an actuating magnet. On the other hand, Hall Effect Type switches use a solid-state output to detect changes in magnetic field strength. Both types of switches are commonly used in various industries such as automotive, electronics, and security systems for their reliability and precision in detecting magnetic fields.</p></p>
