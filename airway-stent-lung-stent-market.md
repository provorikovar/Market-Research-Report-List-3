<p><p>Airway stents, also known as lung stents, are medical devices used to keep the airways open and facilitate breathing in patients with respiratory issues. Tracheal stents are specifically designed for placement in the trachea, while laryngeal stents are used in the larynx. Tracheal stents help in treating tracheal stenosis, tracheomalacia, and tracheoesophageal fistula, while laryngeal stents are used for conditions like laryngeal edema and vocal cord paralysis. Both types of stents play a crucial role in improving airway function and quality of life for patients.</p></p>
