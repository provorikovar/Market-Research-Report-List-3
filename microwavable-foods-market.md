<p><p>Microwavable foods can be classified into three main market types: Shelf Stable Microwavable Food, Chilled Food, and Frozen Food. Shelf stable microwavable food refers to products that can be stored at room temperature and quickly heated in the microwave for convenience. Chilled food includes ready-to-eat meals that require refrigeration but can be easily heated in the microwave. Frozen food refers to meals that are kept frozen until ready to be heated and consumed, offering a longer shelf life compared to chilled or shelf stable options.</p></p>
