<p><p>Thermal imaging cores are essential components in thermal imaging devices, allowing for the detection and visualization of heat signatures. There are two main types of thermal imaging cores on the market: uncooled thermal cores and cooled thermal cores. Uncooled thermal cores are more cost-effective and low-maintenance, as they do not require cooling mechanisms. Cooled thermal cores, on the other hand, offer higher resolution and sensitivity, making them ideal for applications where detecting smaller temperature differentials is crucial. Both types have their advantages and are used in various industries such as defense, security, and firefighting.</p></p>
