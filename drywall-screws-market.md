<p><p>Drywall screws are specialized fasteners used to secure drywall to wood or metal framing. Additionally, technological advancements in screw manufacturing processes are leading to the development of more durable and efficient drywall screws, further boosting market growth.</p><p>One of the latest trends in the drywall screws market is the growing adoption of self-drilling screws, which eliminate the need for pre-drilling pilot holes in the drywall. This not only saves time and labor costs but also improves the overall efficiency of the construction process. These trends are expected to shape the future growth of the drywall screws market.</p></p>

<p><p>Drywall screws are commonly divided into two market types: fine threads and coarse threads. Fine threads are designed for use in softer materials, providing a finer grip and preventing the material from cracking. Coarse threads, on the other hand, are ideal for use in harder materials, offering a stronger grip and better resistance to pull-out. Both types of screws are widely used in construction and woodworking for securing drywall, wood panels, and other materials to walls and ceilings.</p></p>
