<p><p>Implantable Heart Pump Equipment refers to devices that are surgically implanted inside the body to assist the heart in pumping blood. Extracorporeal Heart Pump Equipment, on the other hand, are external devices that help pump and oxygenate blood outside the body. Both types of equipment play a crucial role in supporting heart function in patients with heart failure or other cardiovascular conditions. They are used in hospitals and healthcare facilities to provide life-saving treatment to patients in need of cardiac support.</p></p>
