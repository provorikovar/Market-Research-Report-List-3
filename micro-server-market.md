<p><p>A micro server is a small, low-power computer system designed to be used in data centers and other environments where space and energy efficiency are important. The growth of the market is driven by factors such as the increasing demand for energy-efficient and cost-effective server solutions, the rising popularity of cloud computing, and the growing adoption of data analytics and IoT technologies.</p><p>One of the latest trends in the micro server market is the rising adoption of micro servers in edge computing applications. Edge computing involves processing data closer to the source to reduce latency and improve performance, and micro servers are well-suited for these applications due to their small size and low power consumption.</p><p>Another trend in the micro server market is the increasing use of ARM-based processors in micro servers. ARM processors offer advantages such as lower power consumption and better performance per watt compared to traditional x86 processors, making them a popular choice for micro server deployments.</p></p>
