<p><p>Low Methoxyl Pectin is a type of pectin commonly used in food processing for its gelling and thickening properties. There are two main types of Low Methoxyl Pectin on the market: Conventional (LMC) and Amidated (LMA). Conventional LMC requires the presence of calcium ions to form a gel, while Amidated LMA does not, making it suitable for applications where calcium may interfere with other ingredients. Both types of Low Methoxyl Pectin offer unique advantages and are chosen based on the specific requirements of the end product.</p></p>
